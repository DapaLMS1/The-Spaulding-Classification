Critical Items

  • Must be sterile at the point of use
  • Must be capable of being steam sterilised (or classed as single use only - disposable)
  • Critical items not wrapped must be used immediately after sterilisation
  • Critical items wrapped must be stored so as not to damage the integrity of packaging
  • Must be stored in a clean dry covered area
  • Must have a Batch Control Identification label (BCI) when sterilised

Semi-Critical Items

These devices are designed and labelled for multiple uses and are reprocessed by thorough cleaning followed by high-level disinfection or sterilisation between patients. They are made of materials that can withstand repeated reprocessing.

  • Must be sterilised where possible
  • If not able to withstand steam sterilisation, must be cleaned and disinfected with a TGA-listed high-level instrument grade disinfectant or processed through a documented thermal disinfector/washer cycle
  • After sterilising or disinfecting, must be stored in a way to prevent contamination (e.g., bagged/wrapped and in a closed drawer or container)

Non-Critical Items

Cleaning with medical instrument grade detergent and water is sufficient. However, thermal disinfection with heat and water is appropriate if the item can be processed this way.
